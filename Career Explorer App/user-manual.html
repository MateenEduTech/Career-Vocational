1. Getting Started

The Career Explorer App is a Progressive Web App (PWA) — it runs directly in your browser and can be installed on your device without any app store. It works 100% offline after the first visit, making it ideal for schools with limited internet access.

System Requirements

  • Any modern smartphone, tablet, or computer (Android, iOS, Windows, Mac, Linux)
  • Browser: Chrome, Firefox, Edge, or Safari (Chrome recommended for best PWA support)
  • Storage: Less than 2 MB (very lightweight)
  • Internet: Required only for the first visit; fully offline after that
💡 NEP 2020 Note: This app requires no registration, no personal data, and no payment — reflecting NEP 2020's principle of equitable, barrier-free educational access.

2. Installing the App on Your Device

On Android (Chrome Browser)

  1. Open the app URL or the index.html file in Chrome
  2. Look for the "📲 Install Career Explorer App" banner at the bottom of the screen
  3. Tap it, then tap Install in the popup
  4. The app icon will appear on your home screen — tap it to launch like a native app

On Desktop (Chrome/Edge)

  1. Open the app in Chrome or Edge
  2. Click the install icon (⊕) in the address bar, OR go to the browser menu → Install Career Explorer
  3. Click Install — the app opens in its own window

Running Locally from Files

Option A: VS Code Live Server (Recommended)

  1. Download VS Code and install the Live Server extension
  2. Place all app files in one folder (career-explorer-app/)
  3. Right-click index.html → Open with Live Server
  4. App opens at http://127.0.0.1:5500

Option B: Python Simple Server

Open terminal in the app folder and run:
Python 3: python -m http.server 8000
Then visit: http://localhost:8000

3. Navigating the App

The app has two navigation systems working together:

📱

Bottom Navigation Bar (Mobile)

The 9-tab bar at the bottom of the screen is your primary navigation on mobile devices. Each icon leads to a major section: Home, Explore, Interests, Quiz, Paths, Compare, Saved, Notes, Badges.

🖥️

Top Navigation Bar (Desktop)

On wider screens, the top bar shows Home, Explore, Quiz, and Pathways buttons for quick access.

4. Explore Careers

This is the core content module — your gateway to 20+ detailed career profiles (expandable to 50+) covering Technology, Healthcare, Law, Finance, Arts, Environment, Education, Government, and more.

How to Use

  1. Tap 🔭 Explore in the bottom navigation
  2. Browse career cards or use the search bar to find specific careers
  3. Use the category filter buttons (All Fields, Technology, Healthcare, etc.) to narrow down
  4. Tap any career card to open the full detail modal

Career Detail Modal

When you tap a career card, a detailed panel opens with:

  • Overview: What this career is and its real-world role
  • Key Skills Required: 6+ specific skills shown as chips
  • Education Pathway: Step-by-step numbered journey from school to career
  • Future Outlook: Growth analysis and India-specific opportunities
  • 🔖 Bookmark Button: Top-right corner — save careers you want to revisit
💡 Teacher Tip: Use the Explore section during Career Awareness periods (SAT, Activity periods, or Life Skills classes). Ask students to open 3 careers and take notes on which one interests them most and why.

5. Interest Explorer

The Interest Explorer helps students discover careers matched to their personal interests — aligned with NCF 2023's emphasis on self-awareness and student-centered learning.

Step-by-Step Guide

  1. Tap 💡 Interests in the bottom navigation
  2. You will see 8 interest tiles: Technology, Science, Helping People, Arts & Creativity, Data & Analysis, Communication & Writing, Business & Finance, Nature & Environment
  3. Tap one or more tiles that resonate with you — they will highlight in teal when selected
  4. You may select multiple interests (recommended for more results)
  5. Tap "🔍 Find Matching Careers"
  6. A grid of matched career cards appears below — tap any to view details
💡 Classroom Activity Idea: Ask each student to select their top 2 interests, find matching careers, then present to the class why one career matched them. This builds self-awareness and communication skills simultaneously — both NCF 2023 priorities.

6. Career Discovery Quiz

The quiz is the most reflective tool in the app — 10 carefully crafted questions help students identify their dominant interest profile and receive personalized career suggestions. It is based on career development psychology and NCF 2023's holistic learning philosophy.

How to Use

  1. Tap 🧠 Quiz in the navigation
  2. Answer each question by tapping the option that best describes you — there are no right or wrong answers
  3. The progress bar at the top shows how far along you are (10 questions total)
  4. After the final question, your result appears automatically
  5. The result shows: your dominant career profile type, a description, and 4–5 matched career suggestions
  6. Tap any career suggestion to view its full profile
  7. Tap "🔄 Retake Quiz" to try again with different answers

Understanding Quiz Results

The quiz maps your answers to 8 interest domains (Technology, Science, Helping, Creativity, Analysis, Communication, Business, Environment). The domain with the highest score becomes your career profile. Your top 2 domains are used to match careers.

⚠️ Important Note for Students: Quiz results are suggestions for exploration, not a life decision. NEP 2020 encourages flexible, open exploration — your interests can and will change. Take the quiz multiple times with honest answers.

7. Education Pathways

The Pathways section provides visual, linear roadmaps showing how to get from your current school level to a successful career. Six major pathways are included, all reflecting NEP 2020's emphasis on flexible academic routes.

Available Pathways

How to Read the Pathway Map

Each pathway shows a horizontal flow: each blue node is a stage, connected by gold arrows →. Read left to right. The flow represents the typical journey from secondary school to professional practice — but remember, NEP 2020 means there are often multiple valid routes.

💡 Class 9–10 Teacher Tip: Show the Pathways section during stream selection counselling sessions. Ask students: "Which path excites you? Which first step have you already taken?" This creates meaningful stream selection conversations grounded in real educational roadmaps.

8. Compare Careers

The Comparison Tool enables structured, evidence-based career decision-making — directly aligned with PARAKH's emphasis on informed evaluation and analytical thinking skills.

How to Use

  1. Tap ⚖️ Compare in the navigation
  2. Use the first dropdown to select Career 1
  3. Use the second dropdown to select Career 2
  4. The comparison table generates automatically below
  5. The table shows 7 dimensions: Field, Salary Range, Future Outlook, Key Skills, Top Entry Route, Tags, and Overview

Dimensions Explained

  • Field: The broad professional category of each career
  • Salary Range: Indicative annual income range in Indian Rupees (₹)
  • Future Outlook: Star rating (⭐) based on projected growth and demand
  • Key Skills: Top 3 skills required — helps students assess their own alignment
  • Top Entry Route: The most common first step in the educational pathway
💡 Classroom Activity: Give students a worksheet: "Compare one career you're excited about with one your parents suggest. What does the data show? What questions does it raise?" This builds critical thinking and family communication simultaneously.

9. Bookmarks — My Saved Careers

Bookmarks allow students to save careers they find interesting during exploration and revisit them for deeper study — functioning as a personal shortlist for career planning.

How to Bookmark a Career

  1. Open any career (from Explore, Interest Explorer, or Quiz results)
  2. Tap the 🔖 bookmark icon in the top-right corner of the career detail panel
  3. The icon turns gold (★) to confirm it's saved
  4. To remove: tap the gold bookmark again

Viewing Bookmarks

  1. Tap 🔖 Saved in the bottom navigation
  2. All saved careers appear as cards — tap any to reopen the full detail
  3. Bookmarks persist across sessions (stored in your browser's local storage)
⚠️ Important: Bookmarks are saved in your browser's local storage. Clearing browser data or using a different browser/device will reset them. Encourage students to take a screenshot of their bookmarks list for their records.

10. My Notes

The Notes section is a private offline journal — students write career reflections, goals, and questions. This implements NCF 2023's emphasis on metacognition and reflective learning practices.

How to Use

  1. Tap 📝 Notes in the bottom navigation
  2. Type in the large text area — write freely about your career thoughts
  3. Tap 💾 Save Notes — a confirmation appears briefly
  4. Your notes are automatically restored every time you return to this section

Suggested Note Prompts for Students

  • "The career I explored today that surprised me was…"
  • "My strongest interest from the quiz was… because…"
  • "The educational pathway I need to follow for my dream career is…"
  • "Three questions I want to ask a professional in this field…"
  • "My parent's suggested career vs my chosen career — differences are…"

12. Using the App Offline

The app is designed for offline-first use. After the first successful load with internet:

  • The Service Worker automatically caches all app files
  • On subsequent visits — even with no internet — the full app loads
  • All features work offline: exploring careers, the quiz, pathways, compare, bookmarks, and notes
  • The only thing that requires internet is loading the app for the very first time
💡 School Setup Tip: Load the app once on Wi-Fi (school or home internet) before taking it to areas with poor connectivity. After that first load, it works permanently offline on that device until browser data is cleared.

13. Guide for Teachers & Counsellors

This app is designed to complement structured career counselling sessions. Here are recommended ways to integrate it into school activities:

Suggested Lesson Plans

Session 1: Career Awareness (45 minutes)

  1. Open the app on the class display (projector or smart board)
  2. Navigate to Explore → browse 3 careers from different fields together
  3. Ask students: "Which skill from these careers do you already have?"
  4. Students individually explore 2 more careers on their own devices
  5. Exit activity: Each student bookmarks 1 career and writes one sentence in Notes

Session 2: Self-Discovery Quiz (30 minutes)

  1. Introduce the Interest Explorer — demonstrate selecting interests on display
  2. Students individually complete the Career Quiz on their devices
  3. Students write their quiz result in their Notes section
  4. Group discussion: "Did the result surprise you? Why or why not?"
  5. Connect quiz results to stream selection considerations

Session 3: Pathway Planning (45 minutes)

  1. Show the Education Pathways section on display
  2. Walk through 2 pathways together — ask students to identify where they currently are
  3. Students choose their preferred pathway and use Compare to evaluate 2 related careers
  4. Students write a career plan in Notes: "My goal career is X. The steps I need to take are…"
  5. Teacher reviews notes with individual students for counselling follow-up

Assessment Integration

While the app has no built-in teacher dashboard (by design — privacy-first), teachers can:

  • Ask students to share their Notes screenshot as a written reflection assignment
  • Ask students to share their Bookmarks list as a "Career Shortlist" portfolio submission
  • Use badge screenshots as evidence of career exploration for holistic assessment records

14. Hosting & Sharing the App

GitHub Pages (Free)

  1. Create a free account at github.com
  2. Create a new repository named career-explorer
  3. Upload all app files (index.html, manifest.json, service-worker.js, author.jpg, etc.)
  4. Go to Settings → Pages → Source: main branch
  5. Your app is live at https://yourusername.github.io/career-explorer

Netlify (Easiest — Drag & Drop)

  1. Go to netlify.com → Sign Up (free)
  2. Drag your entire app folder onto the Netlify dashboard
  3. Your app is live instantly with a free URL (you can customize it)

Converting to Android APK

  1. Host the app online (GitHub Pages or Netlify)
  2. Visit pwabuilder.com
  3. Enter your app's URL → click Start
  4. Download the Android APK / AAB package
  5. Install directly on Android (APK) or upload to Google Play Console (AAB)
