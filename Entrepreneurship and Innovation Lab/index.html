Step 1 — Empathize

Understand the User

Empathy is the foundation of Design Thinking. You must deeply understand the people you're designing for — their needs, frustrations, context, and goals.

💬 Reflection Prompt

If you could spend a day with the person you're designing for, what would you observe? What questions would you ask? Write your reflections above.

Step 2 — Define

Frame the Problem

Use insights from empathy research to define a clear, human-centered problem statement (Point of View or POV).

Step 3 — Ideate

Generate Solutions

Brainstorm as many ideas as possible. Quantity over quality at this stage — wild ideas are welcome!

Step 4 — Prototype

Build to Think

Create a simple, low-cost representation of your best idea. It could be a sketch, a cardboard model, a role-play, or a paper prototype.

Step 5 — Test

Learn from Feedback

Share your prototype with real users. Observe their reactions, note what works and what doesn't. Use feedback to improve your solution.

About This App

Entrepreneurship & Innovation Lab

Mateen Yousuf

Teacher · School Education Department, Kashmir

An educator committed to bringing experiential, skill-based learning into secondary classrooms across Kashmir. This app represents a vision to make entrepreneurship education accessible, offline, and student-centred.

🎯 Purpose

Help students (Grades 9–12) develop entrepreneurial mindset, learn business fundamentals, and create real-world innovation projects.

📜 Policy Alignment

Fully aligned with NEP 2020, NCF 2023, and JKBOSE curriculum goals for vocational education and skill development.

📱 Offline PWA

Works 100% offline. No internet, no login, no backend required. Installable as a mobile app. Runs on low-end Android devices.

🔒 Privacy

All data is stored only on the student's device using LocalStorage. Nothing is sent to any server. Complete data privacy.

📦 Installation

Open in Chrome → tap the three-dot menu → "Add to Home Screen". The app installs like a native app on Android and iOS.

🏫 Classroom Use

Share the app via USB or local network. No internet needed. Each student works on their own device independently.

🚀 APK Conversion Guide

Step 1 — Build the App

Place index.html, manifest.json, service-worker.js, and author.jpg in one folder.

Step 2 — Host Locally or on GitHub Pages

Use VS Code Live Server, or deploy to GitHub Pages (free) at username.github.io/app-name.

Step 3 — Convert to APK using Bubblewrap or PWA Builder

Visit pwabuilder.com → enter your app URL → click Android → download the APK/AAB package.

Step 4 — Sign & Install

Use Android Studio or keytool to sign the APK. Enable "Install from unknown sources" on the device and install.

Step 5 — Distribute

Share the APK file via WhatsApp, USB, Bluetooth, or school LAN. No Play Store required.
